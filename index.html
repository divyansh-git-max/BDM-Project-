Business Problems

Two Core Challenges Identified

Analysis of transactional data at The Bread Basket revealed two distinct operational and revenue problems that data science can directly address.

🛒
01

Low Average Basket Size

Most customers purchase only 1–2 items per visit, with an average basket size of just 2.16 items and a median of 2.0. Over 3,352 transactions consist of a single item, indicating massive missed revenue opportunities per transaction. The dominance of single-item purchases — despite natural item affinity patterns — suggests that cross-selling and bundling strategies are entirely underutilised at the point of sale.

📉
02

Sales Variability & Operational Inefficiency

Significant day-to-day and week-to-week variability in sales leads to inefficiencies in staffing and inventory planning. Weekend mornings (especially Saturday, 10 AM–2 PM) generate peak demand, while weekday evenings record near-zero sales — yet the bakery remains open late until 1:00 AM during winter months. This mismatch between operational hours and actual demand causes avoidable cost overruns and resource waste.

Data-Driven Recommendations

Actionable Strategies

Nine specific recommendations, grounded in analytical findings, to address both business problems.

REC 01 — PROBLEM 1

🎁 Launch Data-Driven Combo Offers

Based on high lift values, introduce combos such as Coffee + Cake, Coffee + Pastry, Tea + Cake, and Bread + Pastry. Price combos slightly below the combined individual price. Schedule offers primarily during morning hours when beverage purchases peak.

REC 02 — PROBLEM 1

🖥️ Implement POS-Based Upsell Prompts

Incorporate scripted prompts into the point-of-sale interface. When a customer orders Coffee, prompt staff: "Would you like to add a slice of Cake for just £0.50 more?" This leverages association rules in real-time to nudge customers towards multi-item purchases.

REC 03 — PROBLEM 1

🏪 Optimise Product Placement & Signage

Rearrange store layout to co-locate frequently associated products. Display pastries beside coffee dispensers. Use signage like "Best with Coffee" or "Perfect Pairing" to increase visual triggers for add-on purchases, directly aligning with network graph findings.

REC 04 — PROBLEM 1

🎟️ Introduce Weekday Loyalty Discounts for Multi-Item Orders

Offer loyalty rewards that activate on weekday multi-item purchases: "Buy 2 items, Get 10% Off — Monday to Thursday Only." Uses consistent weekday traffic to drive more value per transaction during slow sales periods.

REC 01 — PROBLEM 2

📦 Implement Forecast-Based Inventory Planning

Use Prophet for weekly stock planning to accommodate recurring weekend surges. Pair it with XGBoost for short-term day-before demand tuning. Ensures adequate stock on peak days (Friday–Sunday) while minimising wastage on low-demand days (Monday–Wednesday).

REC 02 — PROBLEM 2

👥 Realign Staff Scheduling with Hourly Demand

Schedule more staff during 9 AM–2 PM on weekends when sales activity is highest. Reduce staff during weekday evenings where transaction volumes are consistently low. Ensures cost-effective allocation without compromising service quality during peak hours.

REC 03 — PROBLEM 2

🎲 Use Monte Carlo for Buffer Stock Decisions

Apply the upper 95th percentile from Monte Carlo simulation to define buffer stock thresholds, ensuring readiness for occasional demand spikes without overproducing on stable days. Mean simulated demand is ~105–110 daily sales.

REC 04 — PROBLEM 2

🕐 Adjust Operating Hours During Off-Peak Seasons

Sales data (October–April winter months) showed negligible transactions post 5 PM. Consider closing earlier on weekdays during these months to reduce utility and staffing costs, aligning operational hours with actual customer demand patterns.

REC 05 — PROBLEM 2

🌙 Launch Off-Peak Incentives to Smooth Demand

To reduce over-reliance on weekend peaks, launch evening deals on weekdays. Targeted promotions can help redistribute demand, improve inventory flow, and make better use of underutilised staff during quieter time blocks.
